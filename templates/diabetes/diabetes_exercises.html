Diabetes Exercise Hub

Walking

Walking is one of the simplest and most effective exercises for managing diabetes. It helps improve blood sugar control, cardiovascular health, and overall fitness

⏱ 10:00 | Low Intensity

Power Walking for Beginners

A brisk walk that increases your heart rate while keeping it manageable for beginners. Great for improving insulin sensitivity.

⏱ 15:00 | Low Intensity

High Intensity Walking

Alternate between 2 minutes of brisk walking and 1 minute of slow-paced recovery. Helps in better glucose management and calorie burn.

⏱ 20:00 | Low Intensity

Nature Walk

Walk at a moderate pace in a park or trail, soaking in fresh air and reducing stress, which can also impact blood sugar levels positively.

Strength Training

Strength training helps build muscle, burn fat, and improve insulin sensitivity.

⏱ 10:00 | Low Intensity

Resistance Band Exercises

A low-impact exercise using a resistance band to strengthen your back and arms while stabilizing blood sugar levels

⏱ 10:00 | Low Intensity

Bodyweight Squats

Strengthens the lower body and core, improving glucose uptake in muscles

3 sets | Low Intensity

Knee Push-Ups

A modified push-up exercise that strengthens the upper body and core while promoting blood sugar control through physical activity.

Yoga

Yoga is a gentle, stress-reducing exercise that improves flexibility, mindfulness, and blood sugar control.

⏱ 5:00 | Low Intensity

Cat-Cow Pose

A flowing movement between two poses to stretch the spine and improve circulation.

⏱ 6:00 | Low Intensity

Seated Forward Bend

A calming stretch that helps reduce stress and regulate blood sugar levels.

⏱ 8:00 | Low Intensity

Corpse Pose (Savasana)

A relaxation pose to calm the mind and body, reducing stress-induced blood sugar fluctuations.
